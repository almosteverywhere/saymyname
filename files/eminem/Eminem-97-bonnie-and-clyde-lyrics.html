About '97 Bonnie and Clyde

Eminem coos “Bonnie” (really Eminem’s daughter, Halie) into calm as he murders her mom and disposes of her body

The creepiest part of this song is how serious he seems. Unlike in “Kim” where he talks about the same topic, but yells it, Em doesn’t even have to raise his voice, and uses baby talk. The Song “Kim” is also the first part of the story this song is telling — Eminem kills Kim, her new husband and the stepson in “Kim” and disposes the bodies in this song, even though “Kim” came out one year after.

The scene where he plans on killing Kim is the album cover for the Slim Shady LP which came out in 1999. The song is called “‘97 Bonnie and Clyde” as homage to 2Pac’s ‘96 Bonnie and Clyde, and since it first appeared off The Slim Shady EP in 1997.
