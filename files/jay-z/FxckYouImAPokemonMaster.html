[Intro Dialogue from Scarface, 1983]

[Interviewer]
"I've heard whispers... about the financial support your government receives from the drug industry?"

[Gutierrez]
"Well, the irony of this, of course, is that this money, which is in the billions, is coming from your country. You see, you are the major purchaser of our national product, which is, of course, cocaína."

[Interviewer]
"Cocaine. On one hand, you're saying the United States government is spending millions of dollars to eliminate the flow of drugs onto our streets. At the same time, we are doing business with the very same government that is flooding our streets with cocaine?"

[Gutierrez]
"Mm, mm, si, si. Let me show you a few of the other characters that are involved in this... tragicomedy." Immortal Technique – Peruvian Cocaine

The greatest of all gangster movies, Brian De Palma’s 1983 epic Scarface, features this fictional television interview. The interview might be fictional, but it deals with very real subjects. Dr. Gutierrez describes the political scene surrounding cocaine as a “tragic comedy,” and he’s not wrong. The US government is in business with the dictators and drug lords who produce cocaine, but punishes its own citizens who turn to dealing drugs out of desperation or a desire to keep up with the Joneses and accumulate material goods.

The word tragicomedy to describe a play that is both tragic and comedic, or comedic to the extent that it is tragic, dates back to the late 1500s, and playwrights like Sydney and Fletcher, who might today be loosely considered Shakespeare’s contemporaries, used it to label their own works. Tech definitely blends tragedy and comedy together in “Peruvian Cocaine”!

[Hook] Gorillaz (Ft. Sweetie Irie) – Clint Eastwood (Ed Case Remix)

Sweetie Irie is a UK producer and reggae singer, born in 1971, who contributes the reggae lyrics here, transforming Gorillaz cerebral original into a more rhythm-based affair, the perfect number for rump-shaking. Indeed, this was the version that rocked the airwaves in the UK: the album cut was more popular in the US and Canada.

Sweet collaborated with Case again on 2001’s UK Garage hit, “Who?”

Ed Case (born 1974) is a music producer/songwriter and composer from London, England. Having established himself as a professional producer and remixer, he has remixed for Destiny’s Child, Jason Derulo and Beenieman. He is extremely well respected in the industry and continues to have commercial success with remixes and composing for television.

This UK Garage remix of the song by Ed Case and Sweetie Irie (“Dis da man you call Sweet”) features as a b-side to most of the single releases. The Ed Case remix was the version most played on UK radio stations during the single’s original chart run, partially due to the mainstream popularity of UK Garage from 1999-2001.

Feat. Bon Iver Kanye West – My Beautiful Dark Twisted Fantasy (Credits)

THE WOODS & THE WORLD

If we take “Lost in the World” and “Who Will Survive In America” as one two-movement song suite, then we can consider it as being built around two very prominent vocal samples. The second is Gil-Scott Heron’s poem, which we’ll look at further down in the credits. The first is Bon Iver’s amazing “Woods,” which, like Imogen Heep’s “Hide & Seek” and Kanye’s 808s album before it, attempts to use autotune in a novel way.

http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=tZYVJlhnqxQ

“The Woods” appears on the Blood Bank EP, released in between Bon Iver’s two full-lengths, For Emma and Bon Iver, Bon Iver. It’s not technically a sample, because Justin rerecords the vocals for this song. The lyrics are expanded considerably from the original.

Bon Iver in his interview with Vulture, 2010:

“[Kanye] was pointing out lyrics from Blood Bank and said he was a fan of For Emma and really wanted to talk to me about the vocal techniques I’d been using in the studio. I use Auto-Tune in a different kind of way, and he was into that.

Original:

I’m up in the woods
I’m down on my mind
I’m building a still
To slow down the time

Additional verse for “Lost in the World”:

I’m lost in the world
I’m down on my mind
I’m new in the city
And I’m down for the night
