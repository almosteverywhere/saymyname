About 1-900-Hustler

A great song mainly notable for two things — it’s the first appearance on a Roc-A-Fella song by Freeway, and it’s an, um, “updating” of The Convicts' 1991 song “1-900-Dial-A-Crook”.

This banger was produced by Bink! and samples Ain’t Gonna Happen by Ten Wheel Drive and Genya Ravan. It’s also notable for being a keystone in the rift between Bink Dogg and former Roc-A-Fella companion Just Blaze, with the former claiming that the other bit this style and based his future sound on it.
