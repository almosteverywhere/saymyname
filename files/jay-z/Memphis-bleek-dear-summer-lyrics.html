About Dear Summer

This song is a letter Jay has written to the rap game bidding it farewell and reminiscing about his career. He refers to rap as Summer because his most famous singles dropped during summer time so he associates his rapping career with summertime. It is also perhaps a seasonal metaphor (Jay’s rap career was the summer of his life)

This song came out between Jay’s retirement “Black Album” and his comeback album “Kingdom Come.” During this time, Jay-Z had stopped recording albums but still did guest verses here and there

Funnily enough, this record is featured on Memphis Bleek’s “534” album but Bleek doesn’t get to rap on it. This basically never happens, and is probably something Bleek had to accept since he owes his entire career to Jay. (Similarly, on “Anything” off of Beanie Sigel’s 1999 album “The Truth” Jay-Z gets 3 whole verses without a Sigel appearance.) The official explanation is that Jay heard the beat, freestyled, and Bleek loved it as is. But c'mon, who believes that Bleek didn’t want to get on this song…

The track is produced by Just Blaze
