I had that bitch on a rope Jay Z (Ft. Travis Scott) – Crown

Jay’s now taking over the world of Sadism, now who gon' stop him, huh?! Okay, not quite but he is alluding to the now established hip-hop slang of referring to cocaine as White Girl and saying he had control over it or distributed it (roping equates chain supply).

This goes more in line with the previous bars. Made it disappear then reappear, like a magic trick they make a dove disappear and then reappear because it’s tied to rope.

An allusion to basketball: when a player has exception ball handle, i.e. dribbling prowess, they’re said to have the ball “on a rope”. Meaning they have the ability to make the ball do what they want. This ties back to the G.O.A.T. line as this is a term commonly used in basketball, correlating to the theme of athletics in this song. J had the ability to make both coke and his career act according to his own will.

The greatest form of giving is anonymous to anonymous
So here y'all go, I promise this Jay Z – Nickels and Dimes

MY MAN, ‘IDES

This is something that Jay has touched on in an earlier interview in the mid 2000’s. He is basically channeling the great Jewish Philosopher Maimonides who wrote Eight Levels Of Charity

A lesser level of charity than this is to give to the poor without knowing to whom one gives, and without the recipient knowing from who he received. For this is performing a mitzvah solely for the sake of Heaven. This is like the “anonymous fund” that was in the Holy Temple [in Jerusalem]. There the righteous gave in secret, and the good poor profited in secret. Giving to a charity fund is similar to this mode of charity, though one should not contribute to a charity fund unless one knows that the person appointed over the fund is trustworthy.

He basically believes that giving back and bragging about it or telling the world taints the purity of the act. It is an idea that maybe most won’t fully embrace but it’s virtuous nonetheless. Life lessons, L'chaim

SETTING THE BAR

Jay-Z cover has his name on the MCHG cover with a black bar over it. “Black excellence”: the bar, Jay-Z has set it. But like always, no one wants to give him credit. People are also going to persecute him, no matter what. He’ll always be the villan and the enemy, and they’re going to hide and rewrite history, our history, their history, anything to make us look at ourselves in a negative light. They don’t want us to know our rich history. They try to wipe out our history. People are trying to wipe out Jay-Z, and make him blacked out, like that bar… anonymous.

Jay Z – Crown | pending

Jay is an interesting and a lyrically elusive artist. I won’t crowd the annotation but I think it is worth noting this verse from the Bible: God had warned Nebuchadnezzar and had been very patient with him. But finally, the prediction came true. All of the things that he was so proud of were taken away. Remember the fine food at the King’s table that we read about in Daniel 1? That was taken from Nebuchadnezzar; he had to eat grass just like the goats and cows! He was used to living in a grand palace, but now he had no shelter over his head. For 7 years, the great King Nebuchadnezzar lived out in the wilderness like an animal. He lost his mind; he became crazy (Daniel 4:34). Without God, he was helpless.

How weird is it, “turned a man into a goat” line. I think in his own way, Jay dropped MCHG to teach Kanye a lesson. I think Jay’s entire disposition on “Holy Grail” is the antithesis of how Ye has been handling himself in the media lately. It seems Jay feels that Ye needs to take a chill pill cos his antics are frankly insulting based on the opportunity Ye has. He can’t call him out publicly because it will stir up beef so he juxtaposes his own fame with Ye’s response to it to remind his lil brother that “hey dude, it’s just cameras” and when they go away, you’ll miss the fame and all it brings along with it… This is pure speculation of course but my first instinct when I heard Holy Grail and Jay’s samsung pre-release ad for it, I went straight to Ye as the main target of the song…

I used that Bible verse cos IMO, Hov used it as a double entendre to say if he’s the (God) MC and Ye is Yeezus(Jesus), somehow he “made” Ye(God made Jesus). Then he also means in that statement that Ye is being the defiant king and praising lesser gods — Ye called the radio this year to say Lil Wayne was The GOAT, not long after Wayne had taken a shot at Jay & Beyonce — which in itself will be a shot at Jay cos jay will truly be opposed to being 2nd place or lower ranked in the list of GOATS below Weezy…

Seems like a conspiracy theory — watch Elliot Wilson’s interview with Jay — he also then takes his own jab at Ye by saying Drake is his closest competitor! Could still just be coincidences but I think all these artists take subtle shots at each other even Cole on his album Born Sinner took several shots at Jay IMO but I digress.
