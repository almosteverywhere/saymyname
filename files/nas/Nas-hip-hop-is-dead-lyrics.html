About Hip Hop is Dead

The title track and lead single from Nas' provocative 2006 album Hip Hop is Dead. The song and album harshly criticized the state of Hip Hop during a time when the simplistic Southern Crunk and Snap styles were coming to relative prominence. It caused a lot of debate within Hip-Hop circles at the time but didn’t cause any major beef

Over a sample of Iron Butterfly’s In-A-Gadda-Da-Vida this song acts as a sort of thesis statement for the album, decrying the state of the game. Though it didn’t really break into the mainstream it was Nas' highest charting single since the “God’s Son” album
