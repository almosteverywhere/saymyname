About Life's a Bitch

“Life’s a bitch and then you die”

Can’t say it much better than that. Nas and AZ get all existential up in this bitch and reflect on their lives and life in general

As well as being AZ’s debut on wax, this song is notable for the trumpet solo at the end, performed by Nas' father Olu Daru.

The beat is produced by L.E.S. and contains a sample from “Yearning for Your Love” by The Gap Band
