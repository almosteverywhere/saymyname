Word-By-Word Robert Frost Breakdown!

In a good poem, every word is carefully chosen and loaded with significance. That’s the poet’s ideal and the critic’s motivation for “close reading.” And that’s why we’re taking our close reading platform to the limit today with our very first word-by-word annotation of a poem.

Rather than, say, The Iliad, we’re trying this format on a tiny Robert Frost poem: “The Span of Life.” Here’s what it looks like under the microscope.

New Verified Author: Etgar Keret!

Share
6 hours ago

Israeli short story writer Etgar Keret is one of the best-loved authors working today. Salman Rushdie has called him “the voice of the next generation,” which Salman Rushdie probably doesn’t say about most people.

In “What Do We Have In Our Pockets?” Keret’s lovelorn narrator breaks down the many items he’s stuffed into his pants. In today’s Verified Author feature, Keret breaks down the story itself.

His notes are a happy miscellany, a pocketful of stuff he felt like sharing: insights on honesty and fiction, the only thing he won’t do for someone he loves, and one reader’s beautiful animation of the story.

Zora Neale Hurston on Language, Money, and Creativity

Share
14 days ago

This classic essay first appeared in Great Britain in 1934. Though most of the slang it breaks down is long gone, it remains a fascinating look at African-American speech during the Depression as well as a fascinating analysis of language itself: the way in which it resembles money, the way in which its colorful expressions arise “out of the same impulse as the wearing of jewelry and the making of sculpture—the urge to adorn.”
