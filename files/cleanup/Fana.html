"Now it's hard not to kill niggas / It's like a full time ..." (Jay Z – Streets Is Watching) | accepted

In his autobiography, Decoded, Jay-Z explains that these two lines are referred to the time when Vol. 3 Life and Times of S. Carter hit the streets one month before the release as it was bootlegged. After talking to some people he got the name of the guy who was behind it and confronted him at Q-Tip’s solo album release party but as he didn’t have any proof of it, Jay-Z was charged with assault. With these couple lines he tries to explain how hard it is to stay calm in some kind of situation, you can’t afford to commit an error that could cost you your entire career.

"Bin gar keine Russin, stamm' aus Litauen, echt deutsch." (T.S. Eliot – The Waste Land) | accepted

"[Skit] / "I'm gon' ask you one more time homie, where is ..." (Kendrick Lamar – Poetic Justice) | accepted

We can compare this skit to the beginning of the Odyssey.

In the first book of the poem Odysseus finds himself in Ogygia, a remote island, with the goddess Calypso. During a banquet she asks him to narrate his story. From line 170 on, the poem reads:

And now, sir, tell me and tell me true, who you are and where you come from. Tell me of your town and parents, what manner of ship you came in […]

You can clearly see the similarity between the skit and this part of the text.

Odysseus is a hero who fought in the war of Troy and won it but he’s not even recognized by Calypso. He cannot be happy because he’s unable to come back to his throne in Itacha, Calypso fell in love with him and just like Dido in the Aeneid, she doesn’t want her loved to leave.

Kendrick finds himself in a very similar situation. He finds himself away from his hood, his desire of meeting with Sherane can’t be fulfilled because he’s blocked by these two guys who don’t recognize him from around there.

Both Kendrick and Odysseus find someone between them and their desire.
