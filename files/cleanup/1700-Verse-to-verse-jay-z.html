Verse to Verse: Jay-Z

By:
Superflyronald

33,463

Editor
Moderator

Verse to Verse is a walk through all the Biblical verse and imagery referenced over the course of one artistâ€™s career.

We could start discussing Hov’s bible references with his first album (Reasonable Doubt) and work our way to the present, but I have my doubts about that strategy. There are too many albums and compilations to approach this in chronological order. So I’ve gathered all of Jay’s references to biblical verse and categorized them under two modes: Jay-z, The Reluctant Bible Scholar and Jay-z, The God MC.

On the Reluctant Scholar side of things, we have lines like this:

And no, I am not a Christian, though I believe in God
But I don’t believe in the devil, my beliefs are odd
If we the people was given freewill from God
How could He give the people freewill, without giving you E-vil?
My spirit remains vibrant, I will lead the flock
And that’s the tip of the iceberg,
You might hear Christ’s words in my scriptures

Hov lets us know that just because he isn’t religious and hasn’t read the bible (as he confirmed on Fade to Black), it doesn’t mean he won’t quote Jesus in his raps. He also makes a subtle comparison between himself and Jesus, which brings us to other side of the coin: Jay-z as God MC. You don’t have to look much further than lines like “God MC—me—Jay-hova” or “Young Hova the God, nigga blasphemy” to find examples of that. But let’s table the God MC talk for now and address Reluctant Jay first.

Habits of Jay-z, The Reluctant Bible Scholar

  • Sly references to biblical verse. (This is when Hov drops a reference with surprising fidelity to scripture, but in a casual way). Examples:

We playing for property no Monopoly/ So I’ll pass go and let my nephew follow me/ They say that child shall lead

lines up with Isaiah 11:16—

[A]nd the calf and the young lion and the fatling together; and a little child shall lead them.

while this bit from Takeover takes its cues from the Book of Proverbs:

A wise man told me don’t argue with fools
Cause people from a distance can’t tell who is who

compared to Proverb 26:4—

Answer not a fool according to his folly, lest thou also be like unto him.

and one last instance from Never Change:

Word on the street, you reap what you sow

from Galatians 6:7—

Whatsoever a man soweth, that shall he also reap

  • Overt references to scripture (Not from Hov himself, but Pray and You Must Love Me both have guest artists singing biblical verse on their hooks)

On “You Must Love Me,” Kelly Price sings Psalm 138:8.

On “Pray,” Beyonce quotes Psalm 59:1-2, Psalm 59:9, Psalm 71:2, and Psalm 72:17.

Habits of Jay-z, The God MC

  • There’s a reason why they call him Hov (you know, like Jehovah)

Blueprint 2:

Who you know flow vicious as me?
Yet so religiously, that’s why they call me Hov

Breathe Easy:

With the weight of the world on my shoulder
That’s why they call me “Hova"
I’m far from being God
But I work goddamn hard

A Million And One Questions Remix:

Well they call me Jay-hovah cause the flow is religious

  • Letting us know his impact and the consequences that come with double-crossing the God MC

Dear Summer:

Now I’m Pulp Fiction, Colt four-fifth and
Young niggas that blast for me, no religion,

(Note: that blasphemy line was first heard on this freestyle and wound up on an actual album, which is quoted at the beginning of this piece. Also, check out our Pulp Fiction screenplay explanations)

here’s a Matthew 5:5 quoting line from Lucifer:

Yessir, and when I perish, the meek shall inherit the Earth

or the Romans 12:19 line from the same song:

Jesus, I ain’t trying to be facetious, but
“Vengeance is mine” sayeth the Lord, you said it better than all

  • Exploring the similarities between what he has to deal with as a successful person vs. biblical figures who were persecuted

Grammy Family Freestyle (persecuted figure: Jesus):

See Jesus, see Judas; see Caesar, see Brutus
See success is like suicide

Murder to Excellence (persecuted figure: Abel):

They say my Black Card bears the mark of the beast

Hovi Baby (persecuted figure: David, although he picked that fight and Hov is identifying with Goliath in this scenario, whatever):

I have been tryin to be patient with they preoccupation
With David and Goliath

So how do we reconcile all these biblical references from someone who isn’t religious? It reads like Hov’s biggest gripe with religion is its preachiness; he insists that life starts when the church ends, or a line like In Los Angeles like an evangelist, or can’t y'all see that he’s fake, the rap version of T.D. Jakes, or ‘cause the nigga wear a kufi, it don’t mean that he bright. What Jay values is lived experiences, not proverbs or truisms.

It’s telling that those kufi and T.D. Jakes disses were aimed at Nas. You can’t dismiss a rapper as great as Nas out of hand, but you can draw distinctions. This is what Jay did when he spit, You ain’t live it/ You witnessed it from your folks' pad/ You scribbled it in your notepad and created your life. If you’re not living it, Jay doesn’t respect it. (Even if writing and witnessing is a fine way to approach rap. It makes sense that Lupe Fiasco’s—Proust of Rap—favorite album is titled It Was Written).

But Jay has an open mind and a business savvy that is unfuckwithable. It’s his name that appears as Executive Producer of Lupe’s debut, Food & Liquor. That he’d channel all of these biblical verses without reading any of it first makes sense: he’s recorded 14 studio albums, all with rhymes committed straight from memory.“It be that man that write checks, with the hand that don’t write.” There may be no church in the wild, but there’s plenty of room to set up shop.

The first Verse to Verse featured Watch The Throne’s other half, Kanye West.
