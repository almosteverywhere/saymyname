About Barbed Wire

Off of his “(O)verly (D)edicated” mixtape.
Kendrick does his artful rendering of living in the now. Better than anyone has in recent memory.

The first two verses portray stereotypical black males and females, who have shady business fields and are generally thought of as lowlifes, although they are poor and in a bad situation, they go out to the clubs and waste their money away, not listening to anyone who tries to get them on the right track.

In contrast, the third verse depicts a black male and female who have worked hard in legitimate business and taken a difficult, but rewarding path in life. However, the people in the first two verses don’t understand why they are not where the hardworking people are, and write them off as “Members of the Illuminati”, because they are blind to the differences in lifestyle.

The chorus finally ends the song with “a barbed wire in the past”, showing how the hard workers overcame adversity, and deserve to be in the club. Note that in the previous versions of the chorus, the people were celebrating without actually passing the barbed wire- They’re celebrating for no reason, and wasting the money they should be using to get past the wire.
