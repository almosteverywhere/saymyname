About Bitch, Don't Kill My Vibe

The content of this song doesn’t advance the narrative of the album. It is told from the perspective of present-day Kendrick Lamar, who isn’t happy with the state of rap music, and strives to be different by maintaining his vision. He also discusses his career so far and how it has changed his life, for better or worse. It introduces him as the narrator of this album, which mostly contains tales of past events.

The song seems to be directed AT someone in particular, and alludes to private events in Kendrick’s life. We may never know the details of these events, but the themes are universal. Someone is directing bad energy at Kendrick, whether it’s the industry or in a personal relationship (or both).

This song was actually supposed to feature Lady Gaga, as this original version posted on her Twitter shows, but due to problems with his label, she left the album entirely, also cancelling their duo song, Partynauseous. The female vocals are instead performed by Anna Wise.
